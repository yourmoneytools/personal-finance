How to Use the CD Calculator

Our free CD (Certificate of Deposit) calculator helps you estimate how much interest you'll earn over time with a fixed deposit. Whether you're planning short-term savings or a long-term investment, this tool is perfect for comparing rates and terms offered by banks and credit unions. Use it to make smarter decisions and get the most out of your savings.

  1. Enter your initial deposit amount — this is the money you're planning to invest in a CD.
  2. Select the term (in months or years) for which you want to lock in your money.
  3. Input the annual percentage yield (APY) or interest rate provided by your bank or credit union.
  4. Choose how often the interest compounds (e.g., monthly, quarterly, annually).
  5. Click “Calculate” to view your total interest earned and ending balance.

This CD calculator is ideal for comparing high-yield CD options, understanding fixed-term investment returns, and planning your savings with confidence. Bookmark it and check back often to stay ahead of rate changes!

How to Use the Savings Goal Tracker

Our Savings Goal Tracker helps you break down big financial goals into manageable steps. Whether you're saving for a vacation, emergency fund, new car, or just building better money habits, this free tool makes it easy to track your progress, set milestones, and stay motivated. It’s perfect for both short-term and long-term savings plans.

  1. Enter your total savings goal — the amount you want to reach.
  2. Add your current savings balance, so we know where you're starting from.
  3. Choose a deadline or target date for reaching your goal.
  4. Input how much you can save monthly — this will show how long it will take to reach your goal or if you’re on track.
  5. Click “Calculate” to see your projected progress and any adjustments you might need to make.

This tool is great for budgeting, building financial discipline, and making your savings goals feel real and achievable. Use it to plan ahead, visualize your timeline, and track your monthly contributions until you hit your target.

How to Use the Buy vs. Rent Calculator

Our Buy vs. Rent Calculator is designed to help you make an informed decision about whether to purchase a home or continue renting. By comparing the long-term financial impact of both options, this tool gives you insight into potential equity, costs, and savings over time — empowering you to make the right move for your lifestyle and budget.

  1. Enter the monthly rent you're currently paying or would expect to pay.
  2. Input the home price you're considering, along with estimated property taxes and insurance.
  3. Add your expected down payment, loan terms, and mortgage interest rate.
  4. Include details like maintenance costs and expected home appreciation, if available.
  5. Click “Calculate” to compare the cost of buying vs. renting over the selected time period.

This calculator is perfect for first-time buyers, renters, and anyone evaluating long-term housing costs. Use it to understand potential return on investment, total ownership costs, and when buying might make more sense than renting.

How to Use the Emergency Fund Calculator

Our Emergency Fund Calculator helps you determine how much you should set aside to cover unexpected expenses like job loss, medical bills, or urgent home repairs. A well-planned emergency fund provides peace of mind and financial security — especially during uncertain times. This tool is perfect for budgeting and planning your financial safety net.

  1. Enter your average monthly expenses, including rent, utilities, food, transportation, and insurance.
  2. Select how many months of expenses you'd like to cover — most experts recommend 3 to 6 months.
  3. Click “Calculate” to see your recommended emergency fund target.
  4. Optionally, add your current savings to see how far you are from reaching your goal.

This calculator is a helpful starting point for anyone building a financial cushion. Whether you're just beginning to save or reviewing your finances, having an emergency fund in place can help you avoid debt and stay prepared for life's surprises.

How to Use the Burn Rate Calculator

Our Burn Rate Calculator helps you understand how quickly you're spending money and how long your current cash will last. It's especially useful for startups, small businesses, and individuals who want to track financial runway or manage limited savings. Knowing your burn rate can help you make smarter decisions and avoid running out of funds too soon.

  1. Enter your total monthly expenses — include salaries, rent, subscriptions, and other overhead costs.
  2. Add your current cash reserves or available budget.
  3. Click “Calculate” to see your monthly burn rate and how many months your funds will last at this pace.

This tool is ideal for founders, entrepreneurs, and freelancers who want a clear picture of their financial sustainability. Use it to assess risk, plan fundraising, or adjust your budget based on how long your current cash flow can support operations.

How to Use the Monthly Expense Calculator

Our Monthly Expense Calculator is a simple but powerful tool that helps you track where your money goes each month. Whether you're creating a personal budget, trying to cut back on spending, or preparing for a big financial goal, this calculator gives you a full breakdown of your monthly expenses to help you stay in control.

  1. List your regular monthly expenses — such as rent or mortgage, utilities, groceries, transportation, and insurance.
  2. Include any additional categories like entertainment, subscriptions, and debt payments.
  3. Enter the amounts you spend in each category to get a total monthly expense estimate.
  4. Click “Calculate” to view your total spending and identify areas where you can save.

This calculator is great for budgeting, saving, or just gaining insight into your financial habits. Use it to take control of your monthly finances, spot overspending, and build a solid foundation for your personal or household budget.
